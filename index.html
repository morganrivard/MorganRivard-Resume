Morgan Rivard

Acting

As a multifaceted artist, my journey in the world of theater and film has been an extraordinary voyage, marked by an unwavering commitment to the honing of my craft and an enduring passion for the enchanting art of storytelling. It is through this unwavering dedication that I have transformed into a remarkably versatile performer, equally adept on the grand stage and in front of the camera's lens. The origins of my theatrical expedition date back to the year 2015, a time when I took my first tentative steps under the radiant spotlight. It was during this period that I embarked on a journey that would forever shape my artistic identity. My inaugural performance unfolded within the hallowed halls of my elementary school, in a production of "Willy Wonka Jr." There, I expertly portrayed the character of Veruca Salt, the spoiled and petulant child of the Willy Wonka universe. This initial foray into the world of drama served as the catalyst for the budding interest that would soon blossom into a fervent love for the dramatic arts. My quest for artistic enlightenment did not halt at my elementary school's stage door. It was at a Shakespearean summer camp that I seized the opportunity to delve into the intricacies of classical theater. It was there that I undertook the role of Miranda in "The Tempest," a character whose journey of self-discovery and love in a mystical island setting became a pivotal moment in my artistic growth. The works of William Shakespeare, with their eloquent language and timeless themes, captivated my heart and fueled my desire to explore the depths of human emotion through the medium of the stage..

Throughout my years in middle school, I remained actively involved in my school's theatrical productions. These experiences, whether in the spotlight as Rooster in "Annie Jr." or behind the scenes, significantly contributed to my artistic development. Summers brought the chance to further cultivate my passion, as I eagerly attended "Camp Will," organized by The People's Shakespeare Project. Here, I had the privilege of participating in the staging of Shakespearean classics such as "Hamlet," "A Midsummer Night's Dream," "As You Like It," and "Macbeth." Each production enriched my understanding of the nuances of Shakespearean language and deepened my appreciation for classical theater. With each passing year, my fervor for acting grew stronger, propelling me into high school where I assumed leading roles in a myriad of productions. The opportunity to revisit "A Midsummer Night's Dream," albeit from a more mature perspective, was an illuminating experience, highlighting the growth in my skills as an artist. My portrayal in "The Addams Family" further broadened my horizons, allowing me to explore the realm of dark comedy and eccentric characters. While I continued to thrive on the stage, I also actively engaged in the multifaceted world of theater. My commitment extended to the classroom, as I immersed myself in advanced theater classes at my high school. Alongside these classes, I embarked on a journey of self-discovery through the study of dance and choral performance.

These disciplines offered a holistic approach to my artistic development, sharpening my physicality, expanding my vocal range, and enhancing my overall stage presence. As I ventured into college, I had the opportunity to transition to on-camera acting, furthering my artistic evolution. I participated in a UCR-sponsored video project and starred in multiple on-stage productions, continuing to explore and refine my skills in the art of performance. I am looking forward to taking classes regarding my major so that I can further expand my knowledge and acting toolkit
